About the Conference

Bridging Ancient Wisdom with Modern Science

India (Bharat) has been known for its rich civilizational and cultural heritage. The great civilization of Bharat has been renowned from the west to the east of the world for millennia. Undoubtedly, Bharat is known for very rich scientific and technological knowledge that not only shaped India but also contributed to the growth of other parts of the world.

The broad term Indian Knowledge System (IKS) encompasses Vedic sciences, spirituality, mathematics, astronomy, chemistry and metallurgy, ƒÄyurveda, civil engineering, architecture, ship building, Artha shƒÅstra, yoga, art, classical music and more. All these sub-disciplines thrived over millennia with numerous treatises that stand as testimony to the contributions of our great ancestors.

The traditional knowledge with a holistic approach and newer scientific advancements with a reductionistic approach offer a great and valuable platform to build a resilient future for any nation. Reconnecting and revitalizing the Indian Knowledge Systems for the modern world is essential for building a sustainable and equitable world with a moral order.

Our Vision

This National Conference aims to provide a platform for interdisciplinary scientific dialogue by bridging ancient wisdom with modern science. We bring together scholars and enthusiastic students from different disciplines to encourage interest in this field and motivate exploration, research, and possible collaboration.

In alignment with the National Educational Policy (2020), we emphasize revisiting and integrating ancient Indian knowledge to revitalize and understand IKS with a modern scientific perspective.

Objectives & Expected Outcomes

Conference Objectives

  • Explore India's scientific and technological heritage with proper citations and documentation
  • Highlight ancient Indian mathematics, metallurgy, ƒÄyurveda and their relevance in today's world
  • Promote ancient Indian eco-friendly technology and sustainable living practices

Distinguished Speakers

Leading experts in Indian Knowledge Systems

Prof. M. D. Srinivas

Chairman, Centre for Policy Studies

Renowned physicist and historian of science specializing in Indian mathematics and astronomy. Former Professor of Theoretical Physics at University of Madras, with expertise in quantum mechanics foundations and the Kerala School of Mathematics. Has extensively researched and documented ancient Indian contributions to calculus and planetary models.

Prof. Shekhar Mande

NCCS Pune

Renowned Structural and Computational Biologist. He was the Director General of the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR), India, and the Secretary of the Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR), Ministry of Science and Technology. Prior to this, he was the Director of National Centre for Cell Science, Pune. He was awarded in 2005 the Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize for Science and Technology

Prof. Amartya Dutta

ISI Kolkata

Professor of Mathematics at Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata, with research expertise in Commutative Algebra and Affine Algebraic Geometry. Recipient of INSA Teachers Award and the first Satish C. Bhatnagar Award for History of Mathematics. Has been actively researching and teaching ancient Indian mathematics for over 25 years, with focus on Vedic era mathematics.

Prof. Anil Gaurishetty

IIT Roorkee

Professor of Physics and Head of the Centre for Indian Knowledge Systems at IIT Roorkee. Expert in Nuclear Radiation Detectors and Indian Astronomy. Currently involved in developing Sanskrit translators and teaching Indian Astronomy. His work bridges modern physics with traditional IKS knowledge, promoting interdisciplinary research in ancient Indian sciences.

Prof. V. Ramanathan

IIT BHU

Associate Professor of Chemistry at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, specializing in Laser Spectroscopy, Raman microscopy, and Computational Chemistry. His research includes validation of traditional Indian Siddha medicine through contemporary analytical methods. Brings expertise in bridging ancient Indian chemical knowledge with modern scientific techniques.

Shri Raghav Krishna

Brhat

Founder and CEO of Brhat Culture Creatives and Brhat Educational Trust. With 22 years of experience across game development and academia, he now focuses on cultural storytelling, IKS-centered education, and public policy. Co-author of "Introduction to Indian Knowledge System: Concepts and Applications." Leading voice in mainstreaming IKS in contemporary education and policy frameworks.
